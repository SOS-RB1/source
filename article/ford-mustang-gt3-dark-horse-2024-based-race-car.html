Ford Mustang Enters FIA GT3 Category with New Mustang Dark Horse-Based Race Car

Iconic Sports Car Set to Compete Globally Starting in 2024

Ford Mustang officially joins the FIA GT3 category with the introduction of the new Mustang Dark Horse-based race car. The highly anticipated Mustang GT3 is scheduled to compete on a global scale, making its debut in 2024.

The Mustang GT3 showcases an exciting and daring race livery, designed by renowned motorsports designer Troy Lee. This collaboration with Lee marks his first venture into the world of Ford race cars.

Ford Performance is proud to continue its longstanding partnerships with Multimatic and M-Sport, who will play key roles in the production of the Mustang GT3 and its powerful Coyote-based V8 engine.

Proton Competition has become the first customer team to express their intent to enter the 2024 World Endurance Championship season and the prestigious 24 Hours of Le Mans with the Ford Mustang.

Ford Mustang GT3 Takes on the Challenge of 24 Hours of Le Mans

One of the most iconic sports cars in history, the Ford Mustang GT3, is set to compete in the legendary endurance race, the 24 Hours of Le Mans, in June 2024.

During the centenary celebration on the eve of the 24 Hours of Le Mans, the all-new 2024 Mustang Dark Horse served as the platform for the unveiling of the Mustang GT3 race car. Troy Lee's vibrant and eye-catching design perfectly captures the spirit of this legendary vehicle.

By entering the global FIA GT3 category, Ford continues its storied legacy at the 24 Hours of Le Mans, following its triumph in 2016 with the Ford GT.

“Ford and Le Mans are bound together by history. And now we’re coming back to the most dramatic, most rewarding and most important race in the world”, said Jim Farley, CEO of Ford Motor Company. “It is not Ford versus Ferrari anymore. It is Ford versus everyone. Going back to Le Mans is the beginning of building a global motorsports business with Mustang, just like we are doing with Bronco and Raptor off-road”.

New Ford Performance Branding Unveiled

In conjunction with the Mustang GT3 reveal, Ford is also introducing its new global Ford Performance branding. This refreshed and streamlined identity will be featured on all Ford racing vehicles, showcasing the brand as a standout lifestyle choice with a competitive spirit.

The updated Ford Performance mark is designed for seamless integration across various platforms, including car liveries, merchandise, display assets, parts, accessories, and advertising.

Ford Performance has extended its partnership with Multimatic and M-Sport, leveraging their expertise to bring the Mustang GT3 project to life. Multimatic, known for their work on the iconic Ford GT, will assist in building and supporting the Mustang GT3s. M-Sport, a two-time World Rally Championship-winning team, will assemble the powerful 5.4-liter Coyote-based V8 engines developed by Ford Performance.

“For a project like the Mustang GT3, we turned to two of our most trusted partners in the motorsports world to help bring this vehicle and program together” said Mark Rushbrook, Global Director of Ford Performance Motorsports. “I know we’ll all be as thrilled as Ford fans when Mustang begins racing at the highest levels of GT racing in 2024”.

A Look at the Mustang GT3

The Mustang GT3 race car features a bespoke short-long arm suspension, rear-mounted transaxle gearbox, carbon fiber body panels, and a unique aerodynamic package developed specifically to meet GT3 standards.

Prior to its debut at the 24 Hours of Le Mans in 2024, the Mustang GT3 will participate in various GT3 series around the world, driven by customer teams.

Proton Competition, based in Ehingen, Germany, has been announced as the first customer team to campaign a pair of Mustang GT3s in the FIA World Endurance Championship, starting in 2024.

“This is a very important program and an exciting moment for our organization,” Proton Competition team principal Christian Ried expressed his enthusiasm for the upcoming season. He stated that the team is looking forward to the challenges and opportunities that lie ahead. Ried also praised the dedication and hard work of the team members, acknowledging their crucial role in achieving success on the track. With the continued support of their sponsors and fans, Proton Competition aims to make a strong impact in the racing world and compete at the highest level in the coming races. “The Mustang is a great brand and this is an important step for our team. We look forward to joining with Ford starting in 2024.”

Ford Performance is set to expand its racing efforts by introducing a two-car factory race program featuring the Mustang GT3 in IMSA's GTD Pro class. The responsibility for managing this exciting venture will fall under the capable hands of Multimatic Motorsports. Fans and enthusiasts can mark their calendars for the much-anticipated debut, which is scheduled to take place at the prestigious 2024 Rolex 24 at Daytona. This move signifies Ford's commitment to pushing the boundaries of performance and competitiveness on the racing circuit.

Universal Valved Muffler - Tuning the Sound and Performance of Your Car

The muffler is an essential component of a car's exhaust system, responsible for reducing engine noise and influencing the car's performance (torque, power). However, not all mufflers are created equal. Check out the universal valved muffler, which has gained popularity due to its unique features and advantages.

What is a universal valved muffler and how does it work?

The universal valved muffler, also known as a valved exhaust, is a set consisting of a muffler, a valve, and the necessary components for remote valve control. The whole and is designed in such a way as to provide 2 variants of flue gas flow to choose from.Valve can be opened or closed using a remote control, allowing the driver to control the exhaust noise level according to their preferences. When the valve is closed, the exhaust gases are directed into a second pipe of smaller diameter, which connects to a multifunctional expansion chamber within the muffler. This reduces the exhaust volume and changes its sound. The expanded gases slow down in the chamber, further contributing to noise reduction. From the chamber, the exhaust gases pass through perforations into the main pipe. Opening the valve enables a free flow of gases through a straight pipe within the muffler, equipped with sections of holes. This results in a louder exhaust sound and reduces backpressure, which is not beneficial, especially at high engine speeds.

Benefits of using such a solution in the exhaust system

The main advantage of this solution is the ability to adjust the car's exhaust sound while driving. Using the included remote control, car enthusiasts can open and close the valve, adjusting the exhaust volume to achieve either an aggressive or subtle tone that suits their style and preferences.

Additionally, the universal valved muffler can improve the vehicle's performance. When the valve is open, it enhances the exhaust gas scavenging process. This can have a positive impact on the power and torque curve, especially noticeable in high-performance vehicles. If a stock car has undergone chip-tuning or other modifications aimed at improving performance but without modifying the exhaust, the stock exhaust system might limit the additional potential or cause the engine to overheat. In such cases, a less restrictive exhaust system with one or several valve mufflers can be built.

The muffler is made of polished stainless steel, making it more resistant to atmospheric conditions and the harsh environment found in exhaust systems. The particularly high temperature in the exhaust system does not impress it much compared to a muffler made of black aluminized steel. Its durability and aesthetic appearance are distinguishing features.

Installation and compatibility of the kit

The kit is designed to fit a wide range of vehicles, making it a popular choice for car enthusiasts. However, ensuring compatibility with the car's exhaust system is crucial. Therefore, various inlet and outlet pipe diameters are available to match the muffler to the existing exhaust system:

63mm / 2.5 Inch

76mm / 3 Inch

89mm / 3.5 Inch

101mm / 4 Inch

Installation can be done by a professional mechanic or a skilled DIY enthusiast. After selecting the appropriate pipe diameter, the muffler can be welded or connected to the exhaust system using additional screw-on connectors, reducers, or elbows. The kit also includes two remote controls for opening and closing the valve, a vacuum module receiving signals from the remote control, and hoses for connecting and controlling the vacuum valve. The supplied power cable is equipped with a plug that can be connected to the cigarette lighter socket.

Conclusion

If you want to customize the sound of your car's exhaust or improve its performance even while driving, consider a universal valved muffler. With the ability to control the sound level and reduce backpressure, this muffler provides a unique driving experience. Just remember to choose the appropriate pipe diameters to match your existing exhaust system or for any planned modifications.

Revolutionizing Exhaust Dynamics

The Aero Turbine muffler revolutionizes exhaust dynamics by enabling smoother airflow and boosting vehicle performance. As exhaust gases enter the muffler, they undergo rapid expansion and splitting via patented airfoil cone technology, akin to a jet engine's orifice. The resulting increase in velocity and pressure triggers a spinning motion, accelerating the outer air stream as it recombines with the inner stream. This recombination creates a vacuum effect, muffling exhaust noise while amplifying flow, ultimately enhancing engine performance.

Installing Your Aero Turbine Performance Muffler

Transforming your vehicle's exhaust system with the Aero Turbine Performance Muffler is a straightforward process, but it requires attention to detail. Follow these instructions meticulously to ensure optimal performance and a seamless installation.

Step 1: Removal of Factory Muffler

Begin by removing the factory muffler using an electric Sawzall. Completely cut off the inlet tube from the factory inlet pipe connected to the muffler. Ensure that only the original factory inlet pipe connected to the catalytic converter remains intact. Repeat the same process for the outlet tube, leaving only the factory outlet pipe. A rubber mallet, pry tool, and WD-40 can assist in removing factory joints and hangers. Note: Some factory mufflers may have welded hangers requiring professional cutting and welding.

Step 2: Directional Flow Design

All Aero Turbine mufflers feature a directional flow design and cannot be reversed. Identify the outlet port by locating the two rings on the exit tube, as illustrated in the diagram provided.

Step 3: Positioning and Clamping

Position your new Aero unit and assess the optional placement location. It's common for the Aero unit to be shorter and more compact than the factory muffler. If the Aero unit is shorter, you may need an extension pipe to adjust the length. Slide clamps onto the factory inlet and outlet pipes without tightening them yet. Once the Aero unit is properly positioned, secure each clamp by tightening the bolts with a socket wrench. Avoid over-tightening, as it may damage the clamp or strip the nut and bolt. Apply high-temp silicone to seal all connections, and consider short tack welds for additional reinforcement and a complete seal of all slip-fit connections. Ensure clearance of brake and fuel lines, as well as chassis and frame components.

Step 4: Inspection

After driving the vehicle for 25-50 miles, inspect the installation for any exhaust leaks. Address any leaks promptly to maintain optimal performance.

By following these instructions carefully, you can enjoy the enhanced performance and distinctive sound of your Aero Turbine Performance Muffler with confidence.
